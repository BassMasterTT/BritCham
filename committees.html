The Britcham Committees

Britcham Macao currently has three committees in addition to its Management Committee.

Getting involved in any of the three is a way to meet people in a specific area of interest, helping and advising each other and finding that contact you never knew was so close to hand.

Management Committee

Chairman
Keith Buckley

Keith Buckley is the Chairman of BritCham Macao (formerly known as British Business Association of Macau – BBAM) and has been involved since its establishment in 2006. A Civil Engineer by background he has lived in Macao for sixteen years and has been responsible for the construction management of the Sands Macao Venetian, Four Seasons, Sands Cotai Central and Parisian Resorts. He recently established his own company BCA (Macau) Limited of which he is a Director. He is also Chairman of the construction charity – Lighthouse Club Macau. Prior to coming to Macao he has worked and lived in China, Singapore, Hong Kong, Malaysia, Turkey, Egypt, Iraq Poland the Netherlands and Switzerland for almost 35 years. He was born in Germany where he lived for eighteen years.

Vice-Chairman
Eileen Stow

Eileen Stow is a founder member of BritCham Macao and has served on its General Committee since 2006. Eileen came to Macau in 1993, to assist her brother, Andrew Stow with his then-fledgling business “Lord Stow’s Bakery”, established earlier in 1989. Andrew is recognised as the creator of Macao’s iconic recipe Egg Tart. Eileen assisted Andrew in developing Lord Stow’s Bakery into a Macao Brand, now famous worldwide, and has trebled its size since his sudden passing in 2006. Prior to her arrival in Macao, Eileen spent 15 years in the British Music Business working in 2 membership organisations: The Performing Right's Society and the British Academy of Songwriters Composers & Authors. She feels this enables her to offer solid experience in the running of a membership organisation such as BritCham Macao.

Vice-Chairman/Treasurer
Simon Carrington

Simon had a ten-year career as an equity analyst in London with Merrill Lynch, and other leading investment banks. In 2003, he retrained as an airline pilot, and moved his family to Macao. He is currently a Captain, and the senior Safety Officer at Air Macau. He also ran the Macau Bats Rugby Club for 5 years, growing it fivefold over that period. Simon retains a keen interest in finance, aviation, rugby, and education.

Executive Director
Andrew Evans

Andrew is a banking and finance specialist with extensive experience in Treasury and Dealing Room operations, gained over 20 years with international banks in the City of London. He subsequently worked on the opposite side of the fence as a Project Manager and Lead Consultant for a software house designing and installing Trading systems at banks and financial institutions worldwide. He has qualified as an ACIB (Associate of the Chartered Institute of Bankers). Likewise, he is currently a Director of Evans Financial Consultancy, offering professional and accounting services to SME’s and Individuals in Macao. Andrew is BritCham Macao’s representative on the managing board of MECC (Macau European Chamber of Commerce) where he also acts as their Treasurer.

Executive Director
Jill Rigg

Jill Rigg has many years’ experience in China commencing with the opening of a China consultancy arm of her then business in Hong Kong. She was the first foreign woman in China assisting in the development and introduction of foreign organizations to form JOINT VENTURES with mainland SOEs. This was commenced in 1982, shortly after Deng Xiaoping adopted his open door policy to the West and continued over a ten-year period. She worked with senior personnel within CCPIT (China Council for the promotion of International Trade), MOFCOM (Ministry of Commerce – People’s Republic of China) and MOFERT (Ministry of Foreign Economy, Relations and Trade). She was successful in concluding a number of high-level business deals on behalf of both Government and private sector in the Oil & Gas industry, trading and banking industries, and she later was a pioneer of the recruitment industry in China as well as Hong Kong and Macao. She is currently involved in documentary film research concerning the arts, heritage and culture in Macao and Asia as well as media business development in Macao. Likewise, she is a consultant for GBA matters. In addition, she is a consultant for drone technology and other service industries to the construction industry in South China and Asia.

Executive Director
Maggie Chan

Maggie Chan is a Marketing & Logistic Manager at Jardine Engineering Corporation (JEC) – a member of Jardine Matheson Group. Maggie spent 10 years studying and working in the UK and absolutely fell in love with the British culture ever since. She joined BritCham Macao in 2019 to take the lead in BritCham Macao’s Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) program to help Macau local students who are in need.

Executive Director
Daniel Beitler

Daniel Beitler is a recruitment, events and media professional who has been based in Macau since 2015. He is currently an editor at Macau Daily Times, where he oversees daily newsroom operations and writes extensively on economy, tourism and gaming issues. Formerly, he was involved in the events and conferencing sector in London. Since 2020, he has been working with employers to find better ways to recruit top talent in Macau.

Executive Director
Virgil Chan

Virgil Chan was born in Hong Kong, educated in Canada, and is now living in Macao. Virgil started his career in information technology (IT), gradually migrating to his current role as the Managing Partner and Principal Consultant of Synergy Corporation, a company specializes in training, event management, and management consulting. His expertise lies with the gaming industry, providing management and soft skills training to gaming and hospitality operators. For the past few years, Virgil has also been active in providing entrepreneurship training to people who want to start their business in Macao. Virgil is one of the founding members of the British Business Association of Macao (BBAM), getting involved with BBAM affairs since its inception. Virgil’s role is to link up BBAM with the local Chinese community as well as providing various IT support for BBAM.

Executive Director
Saj Fernando

Saj Fernando is a British Architect and Project Manager who moved to Macao in 2015. He joined the British Business Association of Macao in 2017 as the Events Chair to support British interests in Macao and to promote Macao businesses and subjects of interest to the members of BritCham.
