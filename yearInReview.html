Year in Review

Chairman’s December newsletter

Well, Christmas is almost here and whilst countries all around the world are struggling to control the Covid-19, in Macau we remain safe. Let us hope that situation continues.


On a rather cool 4th December evening we held a very successful Joint Chamber Christmas Party at the lawn at “Artyzen Grand Lapa” Hotel. 200 guests joined us.


The participating Chambers were those of the USA (AmCham) represented by President Robert McBain; Australia (AustCham) represented by Chairman Patrick Lui; Britain (BritCham) represented by Chairman Keith Buckley; Canada (CanCham) represented by President Howard Stribbell; and France (FMCC) represented by Chairman Ruger Verschuren.


A lucky draw was held with prizes donated to the 5 Chambers by companies and individuals. On behalf of BritCham I would like to thank AJ Hackett, Artyzen Grand Lapa Macau, Lord Stow’s Bakery and Taipa Village Association for sponsoring BritCham’s lucky draw gifts. In addition, my thanks go to Macau Daily Times and O Media for media sponsorship.


Seasonal musical entertainment was provided by the Family String Trio, whom we hope to enjoy more of during future events.


To reflect on BritCham’s events in 2020, Committee Member Daniel Beitler has compiled an end of year review. Please go to the link http://youtu.be/It3KMbfQrus (with sound)


I would like to advise you that, the Greater Bay Area British Business Directory will be published once more in 2021. This is a comprehensive guide to British Chamber of Commerce Member Companies operating in Hong Kong, Macao and Guangdong. It is perhaps more pertinent than ever, now that the GBA represents enormous trading opportunities. Members will be contacted in due course to provide their company information for inclusion in the 2021 Directory.


December saw us welcome the following New Members:


Professor Mei Wu as an individual member. Professor Wu is the Adjunct Associate Professor, Center for Macau Studies at UMAC.


Doppelmayr Macau Ltda as a corporate member. Doppelmayr, an Austrian company are the builder and operator of the Sky Cab rope way at Wynn Palace.


Looking ahead the following events are finalized:


20th January – Business Lunch: “32.9 square kilometres: The challenges facing Macao’s Urban Master Plan”. Join our panel of experts as they delve into the challenges facing Macao’s Urban Master Plan that is meant to guide the development of Macao through to the year 2040.


28th January – Brew & Buzz Networking Evening.


Karen Gaignon, Sommelier and Senior Area Sales Manager at EMW wines shares insight on China’s wine industry in her presentation “The Growth of the Chinese Vineyard” followed by wine tasting.


As always, I thank you for supporting BritCham Macao and participating in our Chamber’s activities. I also take this opportunity to thank our BritCham Committee Members for their work during the past year. Time is a precious commodity, and we appreciate their volunteering their time for the Chamber’s benefit.


And so as we say farewell to this turbulent year, it only remains for me to wish you a Merry Christmas and a happy, prosperous and virus-free 2021.


Yours sincerely


Keith Buckley
Chairman
BritCham Macao
